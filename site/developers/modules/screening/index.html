Module documentation

Screening

Candidate-set, criteria, suggestion, final decision, conflict, and adjudication contracts that preserve human authority over include, exclude, and needs-review outcomes.

Scope and ownership

Screening begins only after a reviewable candidate set exists. The module owns the candidate-set contract, criteria records, canonical verdict vocabulary, suggestion records, final human decisions, conflict detection, and append-only adjudication. It does not own raw Search intake. It does not bypass Deduplication.

The implemented types make that boundary explicit. ScreeningCandidateSet can be built from a Deduplication result or a locked reviewable set. ScreeningCriteria captures stage-specific inclusion and exclusion logic. ScreeningDecision and ScreeningConflict keep final authority with identified humans.

Gate and ADR status

The local contract is accepted in ADR 0013, and the implemented module follows that boundary. The accepted scope is clear on three points: raw Search traces are not valid Screening inputs, final scientific decisions require a human actor, and unresolved conflicts block downstream stage advancement.

That boundary is stronger than some CLI and Web behavior in the surrounding ecosystem. Those applications may expose additional workflow surfaces, but Core authority here remains narrow and explicit.

Code surface and implemented behavior

ScreeningModels.cs defines the canonical schemas, stages, verdicts, actors, candidate sets, criteria, suggestions, decisions, and conflicts. ScreeningService.cs enforces the authority rules: unknown stages and verdicts are rejected, automation cannot finalize decisions, final decisions require rationale and criteria digest, unresolved conflicts can block downstream decisions, and full-text decisions require artifact evidence rather than local path identity.

This is one of the clearer implemented modules in the repo because most of its value is in refusal logic. The service stops invalid transitions instead of trying to recover from them silently.

Related modules and current risks

Screening depends on Deduplication for candidate-set intake, on Kernel for digest rules, and later on Artifacts for full-text evidence identity. It is also where AI pressure has to be resisted most carefully, because suggestion records are easy to mistake for decisions.

The main implementation risk is workflow bleed from CLI or Web projection behavior. Reviewer assignment, batch management, and app audit tables may be useful, but they are not Core authority unless a later accepted contract adopts them.

At a glance

  • Status: implemented local contract
  • Input: locked candidate set
  • Final authority: human actor only
