# Product Positioning

Nexus Scholar should not be positioned as another AI paper summarizer. Its stronger position is local-first, audit-grade, reproducible research workflow infrastructure for students, labs, supervisors, systematic-review teams, and research groups that need evidence they can reconstruct.

## Position

Nexus Scholar is the scientific backbone for a research workflow:

- preserve search and import evidence;
- keep raw records available;
- deduplicate safely without erasing source sightings;
- require human review where identity evidence is weak;
- support screening and later full-text workflows;
- export verifiable bundles;
- allow AI assistance without transferring scientific authority to AI.

This creates a different product promise than "upload papers and ask questions." The promise is that a review can be checked, replayed, explained, and handed to another researcher or supervisor with evidence and decision lineage intact.

## Users

- Students who need a beginner-friendly path through research workflows without losing rigor.
- Supervisors who need to inspect how search, deduplication, screening, and reporting decisions were made.
- Labs that need local-first operation and durable audit trails.
- Systematic-review teams that need reproducible imports, candidate review, screening conflicts, and exportable evidence bundles.
- Researchers using AI who still need human authority, provenance, and verifiable boundaries.

## Likely Wedge

The first usable wedge should be narrow and visible:

```text
Import search exports -> preserve evidence -> deduplicate safely -> human review -> export audit bundle
```

This path is strong because it exercises the differentiators:

- raw evidence preservation;
- search/import traceability;
- candidate duplicate explanation;
- review-required fuzzy/no-id cases;
- human merge gates;
- provenance preview;
- bundle verification.

It is also demoable. A user can import an export file, see warnings, review duplicate candidates, decide what to merge or keep separate, and export an audit bundle that explains what happened.

## Product Warning

Avoid building a huge invisible Core without a usable workflow or demo. Core strictness matters, but the product needs an early surface that proves the strictness helps real users. The first UI should make one serious workflow comprehensible rather than showing a broad shell with no trustworthy actions.

## Non-Positioning

Nexus Scholar should not claim to be:

- an automatic reviewer;
- a final scientific decision maker;
- a live provider scraping tool;
- a generic chat interface over PDFs;
- a cloud-first literature warehouse;
- a compatibility-equivalent replacement for the pinned PHP reference without fixture evidence.
