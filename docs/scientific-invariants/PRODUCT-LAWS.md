# Scientific Product Laws

These rules are architectural constraints, not UI preferences.

1. A machine suggestion is never a researcher decision.
2. A protocol draft is never an approved protocol version.
3. Approval binds an actor, timestamp, exact content digest, and version.
4. Approved versions, corpus snapshots, and release snapshots are immutable.
5. Amendments preserve the prior state and identify downstream invalidation.
6. Every scientific mutation records who or what performed it and what inputs produced it.
7. Automated uncertainty remains visible; it is not silently collapsed into inclusion or exclusion.
8. Reporting checklists describe evidence that must be shown; they do not define review conduct by themselves.
9. A plugin declares capabilities and receives only an explicit subset.
10. An embedding index, wiki page, cache, or generated narrative is a projection, not canonical evidence.
11. Model context is assembled from authoritative artifacts and recorded by digest.
12. A review remains exportable and verifiable without Nexus Scholar cloud services.
